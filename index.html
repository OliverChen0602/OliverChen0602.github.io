About Me

Hello, I'm Keqin Chen(陈克勤)

I am an incoming PhD student at the University of Hong Kong(HKU). I obtained my undergraduate degree from National University of Singapore(NUS). My research focuses on machine learning(ML) theory, with emphasis on statistical learning and stochastic optimization.

I am advised by Prof. Yunwen Lei.

Besides research, I am passionate in teaching, cooking, singing and playing games.

Research Interests

I am generally interested in ML theory, while in particular my sub-topics under this direction will be:

  • (1)Apply the framework of algorithmic stability to analyze the generalization behavior of stochastic optimization algorithms, such as SGD and its variants.
  • (2)Develop and analyze training algorithms in challenging contexts, such as large-scale problems.
  • (3)Integrate generalization analysis with ML paradigms, such as deep learning(DL).

Teaching Philosophy

I prefer to create a more relaxing atmosphere in my classroom. While I encourage casual and widespread communication during and beyond the class, I believe that teaching is only meaningful if students make their efforts(sometimes, struggle) to analyze and solve the problems. Therefore, no matter how simple/hard problems turn out to be, I will always guide students by giving extensive hints and support along the way, but avoild spoonfeeding at all occasions. Whenever mathematics comes in, my explanation will always be precise and rigourous, while I will always complement with analogy-style and more intuitive explanation.
